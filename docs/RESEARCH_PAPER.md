## 📋 Abstract

Rapid urbanization in India has intensified many passive civic issues like potholes, garbage dumping, broken streetlights, and water leakage in public spaces. These problems are noticeable, but often remain unsolved for a long time due to slow and complicated traditional grievance redressal systems.

Citizens frequently face long forms, unclear complaint status, and uncertainty about whether their reports reach the correct authorities. To address these challenges, we propose **CivicMate**, a web-based intelligent platform designed to simplify and modernize the grievance redressal process.

### Key Features

The system ensures reliability and accountability through:
- Secure user authentication
- After-login citizens can submit complaints by providing:
  - Short description
  - Scene-accompanying photo
- System further reduces user effort by:
  - Automatically detecting platform location
  - Removing need for manually entering address details
  - Enabling faster, more accurate issue reporting

### AI-Powered Processing

The moment user submits their complaint, platform's AI engine immediately springs into action:

1. **Content Verification**
   - Swiftly processes the information
   - First checks the uploaded image to filter inappropriate content using detection model

2. **Automatic Classification**
   - Following this, AI automatically assigns the complaint to the appropriate category
   - Routes it to the relevant department

3. **Real-time Tracking**
   - Throughout the process, users remain fully informed
   - Monitor status of their complaint in real time
   - Similar to tracking an online order
   - Receive SMS updates whenever status changes

### Transparency Features

Platform allows citizens to:
- View issues reported by others in their locality
- Support them through upvotes
- Highlight community priorities for authorities

### Admin Dashboard

On the administrative side, CivicMate provides a comprehensive dashboard specifically for municipal authorities and their staff:

- Visualization for officials
- Easily review incoming complaints
- Spot recurring problem areas
- Assign tasks to the appropriate team members
- Update each complaint's status from "In Progress" to "Resolved"
- Ensuring clear visibility throughout the workflow

### Impact

These features make it easier for:
- Prioritizing urgent issues
- Prevent any complaint from being overlooked

Overall, CivicMate brings much-needed:
- **Transparency**
- **Speed**
- **Reliability**

to the grievance redressal process. By automating:
- Complaint verification
- Category prediction
- Easy status tracking

The platform allows citizens to report issues without unnecessary hassle and receive faster, more accountable responses.

### Vision

It serves as an important step toward building cleaner, safer, and smarter, while strengthening trust between communities and local government authorities.

## 🎯 Problem Statement

In India, as cities are growing rapidly, citizens encounter a wide range of civic issues on a daily basis such as:

- Overflowing garbage bins
- Broken streetlights
- Potholes
- Open sewers
- Damaged public spaces

### Current Challenges

These problems not only affect the appearance of the city but also pose significant health and safety risks for residents. Although several municipal authorities have established grievance redressal systems, these systems often suffer from:

1. **Slow Processing**
   - Long forms to fill
   - Manual data entry
   - Delayed responses

2. **Lack of Transparency**
   - Citizens frequently face long forms
   - Unclear complaint status
   - Uncertainty about whether reports reach correct authorities

3. **Inefficient Routing**
   - Manual categorization
   - Misplaced or delayed complaints
   - Addressed without proper verification

4. **Communication Gap**
   - No real-time updates
   - Citizens depend on phone calls, emails, or physical visits
   - Making them slow, inconvenient, and lacking any dependable mechanism for complaint tracking

5. **Trust Issues**
   - Residents often uncertain about whether their issues are being acted upon
   - Municipal authorities struggle to process large volumes of unorganized reports
   - Difficult to prioritize or verify complaints
   - Further weakening citizen trust
